## Document Descriptions

### 1. v1.4_INCIDENT_RESPONSE_CONTACTS.md (Main Document)

**Size:** ~8,000 words | **Pages:** 15–20 printed  
**Content:**
- Full on-call rotation (names, phones, emails, Slack)
- 3-tier escalation tree (CTO → Stream Lead → Engineer)
- SLA commitments (P0 <5 min, P1 <2 hours, P2 <24 hours)
- War room procedures (Slack channels, daily standup template)
- Post-incident review template
- Critical contacts (Google Cloud, Twilio, SendGrid, Gemini, NOTIVISA)
- Contact card for war room walls (print & laminate)
- Pre-deploy checklist (May 19)
- 48-hour post-deploy monitoring tasks
- Document control & review cycle

**Use case:** Comprehensive reference during and after incidents. File this with incident reports for post-mortem.

### 2. v1.4_INCIDENT_RESPONSE_QUICK_REFERENCE.md (One-Page Card)

**Size:** ~2,000 words | **Format:** Fits one 8.5x11" page (landscape or portrait)  
**Content:**
- Incident declaration flowchart (decision tree)
- Severity quick reference (P0/P1/P2)
- Key contact numbers (CTO, Stream Lead, Google Cloud, Twilio)
- Critical components to check every 15 min (portal, laudo, signature, queue, SMS)
- Slack channels cheat sheet
- Firestore rules error codes
- Cloud Logs quick view filters
- Rollback decision criteria
- War room setup checklist
- P0 incident immediate actions timeline
- NOTIVISA integration monitoring
- Gemini Vision API troubleshooting
- Audit trail verification steps
- Known issues & workarounds
- Escalation decision tree
- Print instructions

**Use case:** Personal card for every team member. Print on cardstock, laminate, distribute. Keep on desk during launch.

### 3. v1.4_INCIDENT_RESPONSE_CONTACT_TEMPLATE.md (Fillable Form)

**Content:**
- Primary on-call team table (name, phone, email, Slack, timezone, verification)
- Secondary on-call backup table
- Supporting roles (DevOps, Security, Tech Writer)
- Internal stakeholders (Lab Director, Compliance, Legal/LGPD)
- External support contacts (Google Cloud, Twilio, SendGrid, Gemini, NOTIVISA)
- Incident communication channels (Slack workspace, video bridge)
- Verification checklist (all contacts called & confirmed)
- Off-hours coverage notes
- Special cases & known escalation issues
- Fire drill schedule
- Authorization & sign-off (CTO approval)
- Print & distribution instructions

**Use case:** CTO fills this out by May 19 EOD with actual team member info. Print, laminate, post in war room.

- Slack & communication pre-config
- Printed documentation & references

- 48-hour monitoring rotation (hours 0–24 & 24–48, stand-down criteria)
- Issue tracking template (real-time updates)
- Post-incident cleanup (if incident / if no incident / closeout)
- Equipment checklist (morning of + 30 min before + at go-live)

**Use case:** DevOps + CTO use this to set up physical war room May 19. Print sections and post as checklists.

## Success Metrics (May 20–22)

**After 48 hours, v1.4 Phase 4 is considered "production stable" if:**

- ✅ **Zero P0 incidents** (or all resolved in <30 min)
- ✅ **Zero P1 incidents** (or all resolved in <2 hours)
- ✅ **All smoke tests pass** (19/19 flows)
- ✅ **Cloud Logs:** ZERO ERROR lines, <5% WARNING rate
- ✅ **Firestore:** No permission denials, no data corruption
- ✅ **Audit trail:** 100% complete (all writes signed + timestamped)
- ✅ **Performance:** LCP <2s, INP <200ms, CLS <0.05
- ✅ **User adoption:** Positive feedback (if any)

**If any metric fails:** Extend monitoring or declare incident.
