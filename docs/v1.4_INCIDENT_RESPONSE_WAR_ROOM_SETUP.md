## 5. Pre-Launch Dry Run (May 19)

### Schedule

**Time:** 2026-05-19 16:00–17:30 UTC-3 (1.5 hours)

**Participants:** CTO, Stream Lead, Backend Lead, Frontend Lead, QA Lead, DevOps

### Scenario: Simulated P0 Incident

**Setup:**

- One engineer presents a fake error: "Portal login failing for 50% of users"
- No actual system issue (staged in Slack, not real)

**Steps to execute:**

| Step | Owner       | Time | Task                                                           |
| ---- | ----------- | ---- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Reporter    | T+0  | Post to Slack: "🚨 Portal login broken — 50% failure rate"     |
| 2    | CTO         | T+1  | Call (CTO's phone to backup): "P0 incident declared"           |
| 3    | CTO         | T+2  | Post to #incidents: P0 declaration                             |
| 4    | All         | T+3  | Join video bridge (test AV quality)                            |
| 5    | QA          | T+5  | Run smoke tests manually (4 login attempts)                    |
| 6    | Backend     | T+7  | "Root cause: Twilio rate limiting, SMS queue backed up"        |
| 7    | CTO         | T+10 | Decision: "Deploy hotfix OR rollback?" → Choose rollback       |
| 8    | DevOps      | T+12 | Execute mock rollback (don't actually deploy, just show steps) |
| 9    | QA          | T+20 | Re-run smoke tests (simulate green)                            |
| 10   | CTO         | T+25 | Post "all-clear" to #incidents                                 |
| 11   | Tech Writer | T+30 | File mock incident report                                      |

**Success criteria:**

- ✅ All team members present & on time
- ✅ Video bridge works (audio + screen share)
- ✅ Slack messages post correctly
- ✅ Team communicates within SLA (P0 <5 min response)
- ✅ Rollback procedure steps are clear
- ✅ Incident report filed correctly
- ✅ Debrief & lessons (5 min at end)

**Debrief questions (ask after dry run):**

- What went well?
- What was confusing?
- What would you do differently in a real incident?
- Do you know who to call if you're not sure?
