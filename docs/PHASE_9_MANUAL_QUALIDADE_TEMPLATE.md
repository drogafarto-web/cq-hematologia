## 1. QUALITY POLICY STATEMENT

HC Quality is committed to providing world-class clinical laboratory services with an unwavering focus on quality, reliability, and patient safety. Our mission is to deliver accurate, timely, and clinically relevant results that enable healthcare providers to make informed patient care decisions.

We are committed to:

- **Continuous improvement** in all laboratory processes (pre-, analytical, and post-analytical)
- **Regulatory compliance** with all applicable frameworks (DICQ, RDC 978, RDC 786, ISO 15189, LGPD)
- **Staff competency** through investment in training and professional development
- **Transparent communication** with customers and stakeholders regarding quality and safety
- **Risk management** through systematic identification, assessment, and mitigation
- **Quality culture** that transcends individual roles and reaches organizational leadership

This Quality Policy is endorsed by laboratory leadership and reviewed annually. All staff members are trained on this policy and expected to uphold its principles in their daily work.

## 3. RISK MANAGEMENT SUMMARY

HC Quality employs a systematic approach to risk identification, assessment, and mitigation across all laboratory operations. Risk assessment is conducted annually and reviewed quarterly through the Management Review process.

### 3.1 Risk Assessment Categories

**Pre-analytical risks:**  
Sample collection, transport, storage, labeling, specimen integrity

**Analytical risks:**  
Equipment malfunction, reagent failure, calibration drift, operator error, method validation

**Post-analytical risks:**  
Data transcription, reporting errors, critical values notification, result interpretation

**Operational risks:**  
Staff turnover, supplier failure, facility issues, budget constraints, regulatory changes

**Compliance/Regulatory risks:**  
ANVISA regulatory changes, audit findings, accreditation loss, legal liability

### 3.2 Risk Scoring Methodology

**Risk Priority Number (RPN) = Probability (1–5) × Severity (1–5) × Detection (1–5)**

| Score Range | Risk Level | Response |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| 1–24 | Low | Routine monitoring; document decision |
| 25–74 | Medium | Risk mitigation plan; monthly review |
| 75–125 | High | Immediate mitigation required; escalate to QD |

### 3.3 Risk Register Summary

| Risk Category | Example | RPN | Mitigation Action | Owner | Review Frequency |
|---------------|---------|-----|-------------------|-------|------------------|
| Pre-analytical | Sample mislabeling | 48 | Barcode system + 2-step verification | Pre-Analytical Lead | Monthly |
| Analytical | Equipment calibration drift | 72 | Daily QC; monthly calibration verification | Equipment Manager | Quarterly |
| Post-analytical | Critical value not reported | 80 | Automated alerts + dual verification | Quality Coordinator | Quarterly |
| Operational | Key staff departure | 35 | Cross-training program; documented procedures | HR Lead | Semi-annual |
| Compliance | Regulatory change not implemented | 50 | Monthly regulatory scan; notification protocol | Quality Director | Quarterly |

## 4. SUPPLIER MANAGEMENT PROCESS

All suppliers of critical materials (reagents, calibrators, controls, equipment) and services (calibration, maintenance, lab support) are subject to formal assessment and ongoing performance monitoring.

### 4.1 Supplier Classification & Assessment

#### 4.1.1 Reagent & Calibrator Suppliers

- **Pre-qualification audit:** ISO 13485 certification OR equivalent QMS documentation
- **Annual performance review:** On-time delivery, documentation quality, complaint rate
- **Re-certification:** Every 3 years
- **Performance metrics:** Batch-to-batch consistency (CV <5% on daily QC)

#### 4.1.2 Equipment Maintenance & Calibration Services

- **SLA requirement:** Response time ≤24 hours; calibration cert within 10 days
- **Equipment uptime tracking:** Target >99% uptime; <1 hr unplanned downtime/month
- **Annual SLA review:** Performance against contract terms
- **Escalation:** Service failure triggers root cause analysis and supplier meeting

#### 4.1.3 Lab Support & Referral Partners

- **Formal agreement:** Scope, SLA, quality expectations, turnaround time, accreditation requirements
- **Annual accreditation audit:** CAP/CLIA/ISO accreditation verification
- **Complaint tracking:** Complaint rate <1 per 1000 referrals; zero critical errors
- **Closure notification:** Within 7 days of complaint closure

## 5. DOCUMENT CONTROL PROCEDURES

All operational documents (SOPs, Work Instructions, Forms, Records, Policies, Quality Manual) are managed through the HC Quality Document Management System (SGD) with strict version control, approval workflows, and distribution tracking.

### 5.1 Document Lifecycle

1. **DRAFT:** Author creates document; marked as draft; not released to staff
2. **TECHNICAL REVIEW:** Technical specialist (if applicable) reviews for accuracy and completeness; feedback documented
3. **QUALITY DIRECTOR APPROVAL:** QD reviews alignment with quality policy, regulatory requirements, and lab standards; approves or requests changes
4. **DISTRIBUTION:** Published to SGD; distributed to relevant staff; read confirmation logged
5. **ACTIVE:** In use; version clearly marked on document; audit trail maintained
6. **REVIEW CYCLE:** Triennial (every 3 years) OR immediately upon process change
7. **OBSOLETE:** Superseded by new version; archived in SGD with effective obsolescence date; historical versions retained

### 5.2 Document Types & Control Requirements

| Document Type | Approval Level | Review Frequency | Retention | RDC Reference |
|---------------|----------------|------------------|-----------|---------------|
| SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) | QD Signature + date | Every 3 years | 5 years archived | RDC 978 Art. 33 |
| POP (Procedural Operations) | QD + Department Manager | Every 3 years | 5 years archived | RDC 978 Art. 33 |
| Form / Record Form | QD | Every 3 years | Per regulation | RDC 978 Art. 33 |
| Quality Manual | QD + Lab Director | Every 2 years | Indefinite | DICQ 4.2.2.2 |
| Record (Evidence of work) | Operator signature | N/A | Per RDC/DICQ | RDC 978 Art. 5.3 |

### 5.3 SGD Workflow & Approval

**All documents follow this workflow:**

```
Draft 
  → Technical Review (optional) 
  → QD Approval 
  → Distribution 
  → Active 
  → [Periodic Review] 
  → Obsolete Archive
```

**Approval Sign-Off:**

- QD signature required (electronic or handwritten scan acceptable per RDC 978 Art. 88)
- Date and version number mandatory
- Approval audit trail retained per RDC 978 5.3

**Distribution Tracking:**

- Staff read confirmation logged in SGD
- Distribution list maintained and dated
- Obsolete versions removed from active circulation within 5 days of supersession

## 6. INTERNAL AUDIT SCHEDULE

HC Quality conducts regular internal audits to verify compliance with the Quality Management System, regulatory requirements, and DICQ standards. Audits are risk-based and cover all laboratory functions.

### 6.1 Annual Audit Schedule (2026)

| Quarter | DICQ Blocks | Audit Scope | Auditor(s) | Scheduled Date | Report Due |
|---------|-------------|------------|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| Q1 | Block A (Governance), Block B (Documents) | Quality management system, document control, governance | Quality Coordinator + External Auditor (optional) | Jan 15 – Feb 28 | Mar 31, 2026 |
| Q2 | Block D (Quality), Block E (Pre-analytical) | Quality planning, pre-analytical phase, risk management | Quality Coordinator + Lab Manager | Apr 1 – May 31 | Jun 30, 2026 |
| Q3 | Block F (Analytical), Block G (Post-analytical) | Analytical phase, post-analytical phase, reporting | Quality Coordinator + Technical Lead | Jul 1 – Aug 31 | Sep 30, 2026 |
| Q4 | Block H–J (Resources, Environment, Continuity) | Equipment, facilities, biosecurity, disaster recovery | Quality Coordinator + HR Lead | Oct 1 – Nov 30 | Dec 31, 2026 |
| Anytime | Follow-up Audits | CAPA verification, targeted investigations | Quality Coordinator | Per schedule | Within 10 days |
| Annual | Full System | Compliance audit across all 10 DICQ blocks | Quality Coordinator + External Auditor | Sep–Nov 2026 | Nov 30, 2026 |

### 6.2 Audit Reporting & Follow-up

**Audit findings documented with:**

- Evidence-based observations (what was found, where, when)
- Root cause analysis (why the gap exists)
- Regulatory reference (DICQ section, RDC article)
- Risk assessment (impact to quality, patient safety, compliance)

**Corrective/Preventive Actions (CAPA):**

- Assigned to responsible owner with authority to implement
- Target closure date defined (typically 30–90 days)
- Effectiveness verification planned (re-audit, re-test, observation)

**Follow-up Audit:**

- Conducted within 30 days of CAPA target date
- Verifies implementation completeness and effectiveness
- Re-audit documented in audit trail

**Audit Reporting:**

- Formal audit report signed by auditor and auditee representative
- Archived in SGD under `/auditoria/audit-reports/[YYYY]/`
- Summary presented to Management Review for trend analysis

**Auditor Independence:**

- Auditors must be independent from the auditee (no direct reporting relationship)
- All auditors must be trained in ISO 19011 or equivalent audit standards
- Audit training records maintained in educacao-continuada module

## 7. MANAGEMENT REVIEW SCHEDULE

The Management Review is HC Quality's primary governance mechanism for strategic assessment, continuous improvement planning, and compliance verification. Meetings are held quarterly; minutes document 15 mandatory inputs per DICQ 4.15.

### 7.1 2026 Management Review Schedule

| Meeting | Scheduled Date | Primary Focus | Attendees |
|---------|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| Q1 MR | Mar 15, 2026 | Prior year audit findings, CAPA closure rate, KPI baseline, strategic planning | Quality Director, Lab Manager, Finance Lead, Operations Manager |
| Q2 MR | Jun 15, 2026 | H1 KPI review, customer complaints, resource assessment, training gaps | Quality Director, Lab Manager, Finance Lead, Operations Manager |
| Q3 MR | Sep 15, 2026 | Audit readiness (pre-external audit), compliance gaps, risk reassessment | Quality Director, Lab Manager, Finance Lead, External Audit Prep Lead |
| Q4 MR | Dec 15, 2026 | Annual compliance audit results, year-end KPI, 2027 quality plan, succession planning | Quality Director, Lab Manager, Finance Lead, All Department Heads |

### 7.2 15 Mandatory Inputs per DICQ 4.15

1. **Audit findings & follow-up status** — Internal and external audit results; CAPA closure rate; overdue findings
2. **Internal and external complaints / customer feedback** — Complaint trend; root cause analysis; corrective actions
3. **Non-conformances and CAPA effectiveness** — NC origin (operator, equipment, supplier, process); CAPA effectiveness verification
4. **Proficiency testing results (CEQ)** — External quality control (z-score, pass/fail); investigation of failures
5. **Quality indicators (turnaround time, error rate, retest %, rework %)** — Trends; comparison to targets; corrective actions
6. **Equipment performance and maintenance status** — Uptime %; calibration compliance; service issues; planned upgrades
7. **Risk assessment and mitigation actions** — Risk register review; new risks identified; mitigation effectiveness
8. **Staff training and competency assessment** — Training completion rate; competency scores; identified gaps; training effectiveness
9. **Resource adequacy (budget, equipment, personnel)** — Headcount needs; equipment replacements; budget utilization; safety issues
10. **Regulatory changes and compliance status** — ANVISA publications; RDC/ISO updates; implementation status; compliance gaps
11. **Customer satisfaction and market feedback** — Survey results; market positioning; competitive landscape; growth opportunities
12. **Strategic initiatives and progress** — Quality Plan milestone tracking; new projects; implementation timelines
13. **Succession planning and organizational changes** — Staffing transitions; key person risk; cross-training status
14. **Procurement and supplier performance** — Approved Supplier List; performance metrics; audit results; issues identified
15. **Actions from previous management reviews** — Closure of prior MR action items; status of open items

### 7.3 Output & Decisions

**Formal minutes must include:**

- Date, time, location, attendees
- Each of the 15 inputs reviewed (documented briefly)
- Decisions made (approvals, risk acceptances, resource allocations)
- Action items assigned (owner, target date, success criteria)
- Quality Plan updates (if strategic objectives or KPI targets change)

**Strategic decisions documented:**

- Budget approvals (equipment, training, staffing)
- New initiatives or process changes
- Risk acceptances (when mitigation is declined; justification required)
- Organizational changes (personnel, role restructuring)

**Quality Plan Update:**

- Annual review at Q4 MR (December)
- Updates to 3–5-year strategic objectives
- KPI target refreshment
- Staffing and resource planning
